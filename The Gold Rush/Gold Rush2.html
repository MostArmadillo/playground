The Gold Rush

People Moving West

U.S Gaining Territory from the West

In the past, Mexico was controlled by Spain. Mexico revolted against the Spanish and took their territories West of the Mississippi River.

After Mexico got new territories, United States got interested in Mexico's new territories and offered to buy California, but Mexico refused. Mexico then lost a lot of land because thousands of Americans known as pioneers traveled West on wagons. The pioneers in California and Texas fought for freedom from Mexican rule.

The war of U.S and Mexico started because U.S wanted California. United States won war and took half of Mexico's territory including California and moved West.

Thousands of prospectors came to California and dug for gold in the hills above Sutter's Mill.

Eventually, ads and newspapers of gold spread across the U.S and the president declared that all these ads and newspapers were true. Then, the word of gold spread around the world!
