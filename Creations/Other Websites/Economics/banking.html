Banking

Banks and Bank Accounts

Banks are used to store money because if you store your own money at home, it can get stolen, lost, or destroyed. When you have a bank account you have it as a checking account, or a savings account. Checking account is used to buy everyday expenses like food, clothes, water, etc. A savings account is money you save and don’t you use until you retire, for vacations, moving to a new house, or anything important or expensive.

Benefits of Keeping money in a bank

If you keep your money at home. The money can get lost, destroyed, or robbed but when banks get robbed, destroyed, go out of business, etc your money in the bank is insured which means if you lose your money, $250,000 will be given to you back. Another benefit to store money in a bank rather than keeping it at home is that banks will give you interest or a percentage of the number of cash you have stored in the bank. So you will be given money by just letting your money sit the bank for a while but the amount of interest a bank pays depends on the type of bank account you open.

Debit Cards

At a bank you can withdraw your money, take money from your bank account, as a form of cash, or a debit card. Debit cards are cards you take away from your checking account and the value that is taken away or withdrawn is put into the debit card. Debit cards are used to pay but they have value so when you use it to pay for something it will lose cash until it has not enough money or no more money inside it. You get a debit card by an ATM to withdraw the cash as a debit card, or you can just withdraw normal cash. But don’t forget the difference of a debit card and a credit card.

Checks

Checks are another way you can pay someone by putting your account information on it and this is normally used to pay workers or make deposits and withdrawals from your bank. But if you take more money if you have in your bank account you overdraw and have to pay a penalty.

Tips on Savings Accounts and Overdrawing

To not overdraw without noticing is too once in a while check your balance. With a savings account, it is much harder to withdraw than normal checking accounts because if you make large withdrawals the bank will charge you fees or if you have little money in your savings account. But the advantage of a savings account is that you get more interest than your checking account so it is best to rarely withdraw from your savings account so you don’t get charged fees and let the money sit for a while from time to time for profit.

Where Money you Deposited Goes

When you deposit money in your bank account and then you withdraw money, it won’t be the same money you touched because when you deposit money, you’re actually giving the bank money to use to invest, give to other people, etc. But you will always get back the money you deposited because the bank owes you and without bank loans some people wouldn’t be able to buy houses and other major stuff.

Bank Loans

The loan officer of the bank will loan money to business as well. But they won’t give money to random people because the bank needs to see if you have a business plan or that you’re poor and they want to donate to you.
