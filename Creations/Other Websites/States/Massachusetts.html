What's In a Name

Massachusetts got its name when the King of England, James II, named Massachusetts after the Massachusett Native American Tribe. The nickname of the state is “The Bay State” The state got its nickname because most of the South Eastern part of Massachusetts is bordered by a bay formed by the Atlantic Ocean.

Early Historical Facts

The Algonquins were the first inhabitants of Massachusetts. The Algonquins’ houses are wigwams, a domed hut, The Algonquins planted beans, squash, and corn. As well as hunted and fished. A european explorer named Bartholomew Gosnold sailed to Massachusetts from England in 1602. The Mayflower ship came with more people from Europe. The european passengers formed a settlement called Plymouth. They needed the help from Native Americans to survive at the settlement.

Political Features

The population of this state is around 7,000,000 people in the year 2018. There are 14 counties in this state. The largest county in size is Worcester, measuring 1,184 - 1,511. The largest county in population is Middlesex with 1,602,947 people. The five largest cities and their populations are: Boston: 589,141 people Worcester: 172,648 people Springfield: 152,082 people Lowell: 105,167 people Cambridge: 101,355 people The major highways in this state are: I-91, I-291, I-391, I-84, I-94, I-95, I-495, I-195, I-395, and I -90.

Statehood History

Massachusetts is the 6th state in the United States. It became a state on February 6, 1788. 5 key events that led up to statehood are: 1.November 19, 1620 Pilgrims land at Plymouth rock. 2. November 25, 1621 Pilgrims celebrate first Thanksgiving. 3. 1773, Boston Tea Party 4.1775, Battles of Lexington and Concord 5. U.S constitution is written

Government

The governor of this state is Charlie Baker. His term will end in November 4, 2022. This state has 9 U.S. Representatives and 2 U.S. Senators. The names of this state’s U.S. Senators are: Elizabeth Warren. Her term will end in 2025. Edward Markey. His term will end in 2025. This state has 11 electoral votes. A fact relating to the state’s government is that Massachusetts adopted the oldest constitution in the U.S on June 16, 1780. The Massachusetts constitution took place before the U.s constitution did in 1787!

Points of Interest

Boston has a trail called the Freedom Trail because it leads to many historic points. The trail can take you to many sites that are important to the history of Boston’s African Americans and Irish Americans. The start of the Freedom Trail is at Boston Common. It is about 50 acres of open land near the center of Boston’s Business District. Further down the Freedom Trail is the State House. There is also Cape Cod, a national seashore. The most important point of interest is Plymouth rock because that is where the Pilgrims landed in 1620.

Interesting Information

In 1620, the pilgrims landed in Plymouth Massachusetts and set up a settlement there. In 1716 Boston Harbor was home to the first American Lighthouse. Many events that led up to the American Revolution happened in Massachusetts like the Boston Tea Party or Boston Massacre. Dunkin Donuts first opened in Massachusetts so it is very common in the Northeastern of U.S.
