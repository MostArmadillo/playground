How the Gold Rush Ended

Finally, there was no more gold, most boomtowns became “ghost towns,” towns that are abandoned. Some miners made $300 worth of gold each day although most miners made $30 a day. Some miners got enough gold to live in their home country for several years. Some boomtowns are still cities today like San Francisco. Since miners made $30 a day, they saw that eventually, all their money disappeared! An average meal in the U.S cost $20, boots cost $20 a pair, and eggs cost 50 cents each. Some miners spent money on bad habits like drinking or gambling. The richest people from the gold rush were not the miners, Sam Brannam sold mining supplies and became a millionaire. I think the gold rush brought California to what it is today like San Francisco. It also brought California its nickname, The Golden State.

Conclusion

This is how the Gold Rush is important to California, and Westward Expansion. Without the Gold Rush, California wouldn’t be the same. San Francisco and other cities would be different. The Gold Rush Probably caused a huge effect on the West and created an impact on California.
