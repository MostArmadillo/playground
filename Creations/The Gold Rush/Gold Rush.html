How the Gold Rush Started

Gold was discovered at Sutter's Mill 1849. Sutter's Mill is located in California. It happened when James Marshall was building a sawmill for John Sutter, the owner of Sutter's Mill, and discovered gold in the nearby river. The discovery of gold was meant to be a secret but word spread fast!

The result of the word of gold spreading was: Mining methods polluted the land. Native Americans, African Americans, and other foreign miners were treated unequally. Native Americans suffered from plagues from Asia and Europe. They had no resistance to the plagues of the east.

Before the gold rush, Spanish soldiers searched for a "city of gold" near a river that stretched from the Pacific to Alantic Coast. Although, the "city of gold" or the river didn't exist, causing the soldiers to die from starvation.
